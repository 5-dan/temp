# Brand Voice — Iníciate.us

## Who is speaking

The voice belongs to Daniel, the founder. He is bilingual, writes Spanish with natural ESL patterns, and communicates like a knowledgeable friend — not a financial institution. The tone is direct, warm, and practical.

If a piece reads like a generic financial influencer, a bank, or a nonprofit rescue program, it failed.

---

## Core tone principles

**Direct.** Say the thing. No hedging, no preamble, no throat-clearing.

**Warm but not soft.** We care about the reader. We do not coddle them or imply they need saving.

**Practical.** Every piece should leave the reader with something they can do or understand. Abstract inspiration is not our style.

**Honest about timelines.** Credit building takes months. We never promise fast results.

**Treats the audience as capable adults.** Our reader is building a life in a new country. They are resourceful, not helpless.

---

## Grammar and punctuation rules

These are non-negotiable in Spanish output:

- **Short sentences.** One idea per sentence. Split long thoughts.
- **No em dashes (—).** Use a period or rewrite.
- **No semicolons.** Use a period or a conjunction.
- **Sparse Oxford commas.** Avoid unless genuinely needed for clarity.
- **No exclamation overuse.** One per piece maximum, only if it earns it.
- **Paragraph breaks often.** Two to three sentences max per paragraph in social copy.

---

## Prohibited words and phrases

Never use these when describing the audience or their situation:

| Prohibited | Why |
|---|---|
| invisible | Defines them by absence |
| fantasma | Same |
| sin nada | Implies lack, not beginning |
| no existes | Shame framing |
| problema | Positions their situation as something wrong |
| fracaso | Never implied, never stated |
| riesgo | Fear framing |
| peligro | Fear framing |
| mal crédito | Labeling, not describing |

Also avoid:

- Scarcity language: "última oportunidad", "no te quedes atrás"
- Urgency manipulation: "¡ACTÚA AHORA!", "estás perdiendo dinero cada día"
- Rescue framing: "te ayudamos a salir", "no estás solo en esto" (implies they were lost)

## Preferred vocabulary

| Instead of | Use |
|---|---|
| mal crédito | sin historial, empezando desde cero |
| problema de crédito | punto de partida |
| te ayudamos | aquí está cómo funciona |
| fácil y rápido | paso a paso, en 90 días |

## What we are and are not

**We are:** A financial education resource. We explain how credit works, compare options, and share real experiences.

**We are not:** Credit repair. Legal advisors. Investment advisors. Coaches. We do not fix anything for anyone.

**We do not:** Recommend specific brand names in editorial content. Use "tarjeta asegurada", "credit-builder loan", "cuenta de cheques" — never "Chime", "Capital One", etc.

## Register by format

### Instagram captions
- 150-220 words
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- One concrete dollar example or verifiable stat
- CTA at the end: "→ iniciate.us"
- 15 hashtags after the caption body
- Language: Spanish

### Carousel slides
- 5 slides: Hook → Context → Mechanism → Action → Closing
- Max 8 words of visible text per slide
- One idea per slide, one visual element
- No logo on slides (logo is in bio)
- Slide types: STAT, QUOTE, TIMELINE, STEPS, MYTH, CLOSING

### Articles / blog posts
- Open with a hook the reader would actually stop for
- Concrete dollar example or verifiable stat in first 3 paragraphs
- Structure: recognition → mechanism → action → close
- Prose-first, scannable subheaders
- End with one clear next step

### Emails
- Conversational, like a message from Daniel
- Short paragraphs
- One clear action per email
- No hard sells

## On-voice vs off-voice examples

**Off-voice:**
> "Si tienes mal crédito, no te preocupes — ¡estás en el lugar correcto! Nosotros te ayudamos a salir de esa situación difícil con nuestro sistema probado."

Problems: prohibited words (mal crédito), rescue framing (te ayudamos a salir), vague promise (sistema probado), exclamation.

**On-voice:**
> "Cuando llegas a Estados Unidos, tu historial de crédito empieza en cero. No es un obstáculo — es un punto de partida. Aquí te explico cómo funciona el sistema y qué puedes hacer en los primeros 90 días."

Why it works: no prohibited words, no rescue framing, honest about starting point, concrete timeframe.

---

**Off-voice:**
> "¡ÚLTIMA OPORTUNIDAD! Descarga nuestra guía gratis antes de que sea tarde. No pierdas más dinero por no conocer tu score."

Problems: scarcity, urgency, fear, exclamation abuse.

**On-voice:**
> "Esta guía explica cómo funciona el crédito en Estados Unidos: paso a paso, en 90 días. Es gratis. → iniciate.us"

## Final test

Before publishing any piece, ask:

> Does this leave the reader more capable, more informed, and more in control — or more anxious, more confused, or more dependent on the brand?

If the answer is the second half, rewrite it.
